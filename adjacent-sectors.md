# Adjacent Sectors — Expansion Beyond F&B

## The Logic: What Makes a Sector "Adjacent"?

Before listing sectors, let's define what structural characteristics make F&B wholesale painful — and therefore what to look for in adjacent markets. Our agent's value proposition works when a sector has **all five** of these traits:

| Trait | Why it matters | F&B benchmark |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| **1. Fragmented market** | Many mid-size players = large addressable market of underserved companies | 414,000 businesses in EU |
| **2. Multi-channel ordering chaos** | Phone, fax, email, WhatsApp orders = our parsing agent shines | 48% still on spreadsheets |
| **3. Thin margins + labour pressure** | They *need* automation but can't afford enterprise tools | 1–5% margins, acute labour shortage |
| **4. Perishability or time-sensitivity** | Creates urgency; mistakes are expensive; proactive agents add clear value | Fresh produce, dairy, meat |
| **5. Recurring B2B relationships** | Regular orders from known customers = patterns the agent can learn | Restaurants order weekly from same wholesaler |

## Sector-by-Sector Analysis

### A. HoReCa Non-Food Supplies (Hospitality Equipment, Tableware, Cleaning, Amenities)

**Pain-point match: ★★★★★**

| Trait | Score | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Fragmented | ★★★★★ | Thousands of regional distributors across EU — tableware, cleaning chemicals, hotel amenities, kitchen equipment |
| Multi-channel chaos | ★★★★★ | Hotels and restaurants order supplies the same way they order food: phone, email, WhatsApp. Often the *same person* placing both orders |
| Thin margins | ★★★★☆ | Margins slightly better than food (5–15%) but still under pressure |
| Time-sensitivity | ★★★☆☆ | Not perishable, but stockouts are operationally critical (no napkins = no service) |
| Recurring B2B | ★★★★★ | Hotels/restaurants reorder cleaning supplies, amenities, disposables on predictable cycles |

**Why this is the #1 adjacent sector:**
- **Same customer, different SKU.** The restaurant already buying food from a wholesaler also buys cleaning supplies, packaging, disposables, and kitchen equipment from a *different* wholesaler with the *same* operational mess. Our agent can serve both.
- **Cross-sell opportunity:** If you're already processing food orders for a restaurant, you can process their non-food supply orders too — same agent, new revenue stream.
- **Example companies:** [CAUSA HORECA](https://www.causahoreca.com/en/home), regional HoReCa supply distributors in DACH.

### B. Wine, Spirits & Specialty Beverages Distribution

**Pain-point match: ★★★★★**

| Trait | Score | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Fragmented | ★★★★★ | Spirits and wine segments are highly fragmented; many regional importers/distributors |
| Multi-channel chaos | ★★★★★ | Wine merchants, bars, restaurants order by phone/email; catalogues are often PDFs or paper |
| Thin margins | ★★★★☆ | ~20% markup but high competition; regulatory complexity eats margin |
| Time-sensitivity | ★★★☆☆ | Not perishable per se, but seasonal demand swings (Christmas, summer) require proactive planning |
| Recurring B2B | ★★★★★ | Bars and restaurants have standing relationships with wine/spirits distributors |

**Why this is compelling:**
- **Regulatory complexity = agent advantage.** Alcohol distribution involves excise duties, licensing, and country-specific labelling rules. An agent that handles compliance documentation alongside ordering is a massive time-saver.
- **Natural extension from F&B.** Many F&B wholesalers already carry beverages; standalone wine/spirits distributors are a natural adjacent customer.
- **Premium segment = higher willingness to pay.** Wine/spirits distributors often serve premium hospitality; they're less price-sensitive than commodity food wholesalers.
- **Example companies:** [Moving Spirits](https://movingspirits.eu/) (NL), [Prime Spirits Supply](https://primespirits.eu/), regional Getränkegroßhandel across DACH.

### C. Building Materials & Construction Supplies Distribution

**Pain-point match: ★★★★☆**

| Trait | Score | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Fragmented | ★★★★☆ | Highly regional; many mid-size distributors serving local contractors |
| Multi-channel chaos | ★★★★★ | Sales reps manually process quotes → orders → invoices in ERP; massive manual overhead |
| Thin margins | ★★★★☆ | Competitive; pricing policies are chaotic (each rep has their own) |
| Time-sensitivity | ★★★★☆ | Construction delays from parts waiting cost thousands/day |
| Recurring B2B | ★★★★☆ | Contractors have regular suppliers, but orders are more project-based than cyclical |

**Why this is interesting:**
- **Massive market.** EU construction materials distribution is a multi-hundred-billion euro market.
- **Quoting pain is extreme.** Sales reps spend hours building quotes for complex multi-product orders. An agent that auto-generates quotes from specs/BOMs is an instant sell.
- **Higher ACV potential.** Larger ticket sizes per order → more value per automated transaction → can charge more.
- **Caveat:** Orders are more complex (specifications, tolerances, certifications) than F&B. Requires deeper product knowledge in the agent. This makes it a Phase 2 expansion, not a Phase 1 beachhead.
- **Example companies:** Regional Baustoffhandel in DACH, [EDIN network](https://edin-europe.com/) members.

### D. Industrial Parts, Fasteners & MRO (Maintenance, Repair, Operations)

**Pain-point match: ★★★★☆**

| Trait | Score | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Fragmented | ★★★★☆ | Many regional distributors in EU; niche specialists by product type |
| Multi-channel chaos | ★★★★☆ | Phone/email ordering common; many legacy ERPs; "out-of-control inventory" from manual entry |
| Thin margins | ★★★★★ | Scale business; manual errors in ordering/inventory directly erode margins |
| Time-sensitivity | ★★★★☆ | Factory downtime from missing parts is extremely expensive |
| Recurring B2B | ★★★★☆ | Factories/workshops have regular MRO suppliers; reorders are predictable |

**Why this is interesting:**
- **SKU complexity is a moat.** Fastener distributors manage thousands of SKUs across sizes, materials, and certifications. An agent that can parse a messy order ("200 M8x40 hex bolts, zinc plated, DIN 933, and maybe 50 washers to match") and map it correctly is enormously valuable.
- **Legacy ERP pain is acute.** Multiple vendors (Epicor, Ximple, INxSQL) are selling ERP replacements to this segment — proving the pain exists and willingness to pay is there.
- **Caveat:** Technical product knowledge requirements are high. Agent needs to understand part specifications, compatibility, and standards. Harder than food SKUs.
- **Example companies:** Regional fastener/MRO distributors in Germany ([11+ listed on Powertelcom](https://www.powertelcom.com/top-fastener-suppliers-germany/)).

### E. Packaging & Disposables Distribution

**Pain-point match: ★★★★☆**

| Trait | Score | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Fragmented | ★★★★★ | 400+ regional converters in EU flexible packaging alone; many small distributors |
| Multi-channel chaos | ★★★★☆ | B2B ordering via phone/email; catalogues are complex |
| Thin margins | ★★★★☆ | Competitive; sustainability regulation adding compliance costs |
| Time-sensitivity | ★★★☆☆ | Not perishable, but just-in-time delivery is standard |
| Recurring B2B | ★★★★★ | Food producers, restaurants, e-commerce businesses reorder packaging on tight schedules |

**Why this is interesting:**
- **Strong F&B overlap.** F&B wholesalers' customers (restaurants, caterers) also buy packaging, disposables, takeaway containers. Same buyer persona.
- **Regulatory tailwind.** EU Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation is forcing wholesalers to track, report, and adapt — an agent that handles compliance alongside ordering is differentiated.
- **Example companies:** Small family-owned packaging distributors supplying to 600+ HORECA businesses, [Macfarlane Packaging](https://macfarlanepackaging.com/) (regional EU distribution).

### F. Pet Food & Animal Supplies Distribution

**Pain-point match: ★★★☆☆**

| Trait | Score | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Fragmented | ★★★★☆ | Top 5 companies hold only 25.66% of EU market; many independent distributors |
| Multi-channel chaos | ★★★☆☆ | More digitised than F&B (online retail is dominant channel) |
| Thin margins | ★★★☆☆ | Premium segment growing at 7.2% CAGR; margins healthier than commodity food |
| Time-sensitivity | ★★★☆☆ | Some perishable (fresh/frozen pet food) but mostly shelf-stable |
| Recurring B2B | ★★★★☆ | Pet shops, vet clinics, kennels have regular reorder patterns |

**Why this is lower priority:**
- Market is already more digitised than F&B wholesale (online retail dominant).
- Less manual ordering chaos to solve — the pain is real but less acute.
- Good long-term expansion market (€32.5B by 2033), but not the urgent beachhead.

### G. Floral & Fresh Produce (Non-Food)

**Pain-point match: ★★★☆☆**

| Trait | Score | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Fragmented | ★★★★☆ | Many regional wholesalers outside the Dutch auction system |
| Multi-channel chaos | ★★★☆☆ | Dutch auctions are digitised; but regional distributors to florists still use phone/fax |
| Thin margins | ★★★★★ | Extremely thin; perishability means waste = margin destruction |
| Time-sensitivity | ★★★★★ | 24–72 hour shelf life for cut flowers; most perishable product category |
| Recurring B2B | ★★★★☆ | Florists order multiple times per week from wholesalers |

**Why this is lower priority:**
- The extreme perishability makes the agent's accuracy requirements very high from day one — errors are costly and visible.
- The Dutch auction system (FloraHolland) has already digitised the main distribution bottleneck.
- Niche market; smaller TAM than other sectors.

### H. Pharma & Medical Supplies Distribution

**Pain-point match: ★★☆☆☆ (for now)**

| Trait | Score | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Fragmented | ★★★★☆ | 750+ pharmaceutical wholesalers in 33 EU countries (GIRP members) |
| Multi-channel chaos | ★★★☆☆ | More regulated and digitised than F&B; EDI is common |
| Thin margins | ★★★★☆ | Heavily regulated margins in many EU countries |
| Time-sensitivity | ★★★★★ | Life-critical; no room for error |
| Recurring B2B | ★★★★★ | Pharmacies, hospitals reorder on strict schedules |

**Why NOT now:**
- **Regulatory barrier is enormous.** Medical/pharma distribution requires certifications (GDP, GMP), auditable trails, and regulatory approvals for any software touching the supply chain.
- **Error tolerance is zero.** Unlike food (where a wrong item is annoying), a wrong medical supply can be dangerous.
- **Long-term play only.** Once our agent is battle-tested in F&B, the accuracy and auditability could translate — but this is a 3–5 year expansion, not a beachhead.

## The "Platform Thesis" — Why This Expansion Path Matters

The real play isn't building N vertical agents. It's building a **horizontal agentic procurement platform** that happens to start in F&B:

1. **Phase 1:** Nail the core — multi-channel order parsing, smart procurement, proactive customer comms. All built for F&B but architecturally vertical-agnostic.
2. **Phase 2:** Swap the product catalogue and domain rules. The agent already knows how to parse emails, match SKUs, generate POs, and manage supplier relationships. Wine/spirits and HoReCa non-food are trivial extensions because the *buyer persona is the same* (restaurant/hotel manager).
3. **Phase 3:** The same platform serves any mid-size B2B distributor. The moat becomes the **agent's operational intelligence** (patterns learned across thousands of distributor workflows) and the **network effects** (cross-distributor procurement insights).

**This is the story for investors:** "We're not building an F&B tool. We're building the operating system for the 414,000 mid-size B2B distributors in Europe who are running on Excel and phone calls. F&B is just where we prove it works."
